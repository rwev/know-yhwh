> "I will establish my covenant between me and you and your offspring after you throughout their generations for an everlasting covenant, to be God to you and to your offspring after you." — Genesis 17:7

A covenant is a solemn, binding agreement between two parties. The covenants of Scripture form the backbone of God's redemptive plan — each one building on the last, all finding their fulfillment in Christ.

## Key Covenants

- [**Noahic Covenant**](/know-yhwh/covenants/noahic/) — God's promise to preserve creation after the flood
- [**Abrahamic Covenant**](/know-yhwh/covenants/abrahamic/) — Promise of land, seed, and blessing
- [**Mosaic Covenant**](/know-yhwh/covenants/mosaic/) — Law given at Sinai
- [**Davidic Covenant**](/know-yhwh/covenants/davidic/) — Promise of an eternal throne and kingdom
- [**New Covenant**](/know-yhwh/covenants/new-covenant/) — Fulfilled in Christ's blood
- [**Covenant Theology**](/know-yhwh/covenants/covenant-theology/) — How the covenants relate to each other

## The Covenant Thread

From Adam to Christ, God's covenants reveal a single, unfolding plan: to create a people for Himself, dwell among them, and bless all nations through the promised Seed (Galatians 3:16).
