> "He is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation. For by him all things were created, in heaven and on earth, visible and invisible." — Colossians 1:15–16

Jesus Christ is the central figure of all Scripture. The Old Testament points forward to Him; the New Testament reveals Him. He is fully God and fully man — the eternal Son who took on human flesh to redeem a fallen world.

## Christ in All of Scripture

> "And beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, he interpreted to them in all the Scriptures the things concerning himself." — Luke 24:27

Every page of Scripture ultimately points to Jesus — from the seed of the woman in Genesis 3:15, to the Lamb of God in Revelation, He is the thread that holds the entire biblical narrative together.
